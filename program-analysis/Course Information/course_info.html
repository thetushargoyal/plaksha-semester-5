Course Prerequisite(s): 

Background in Programming, including C. For the project, you will use C++.

Textbook(s) 

Andrew Appel, Modern Compiler Implementation in C, Cambridge University Press, 1998 (Hardcover)

or 2004 (Paperback). (You can get either version of the book since they are identical in content and even

page numbers.  There is only one edition of this book, so it is not possible to get the wrong one.)

Topics:

Topics:

·         Compiler introduction

·         The compilation, linking & loading process.

·         Lexical analysis and finite automata

·        Parsing and context-free grammars

 

·        Abstract Syntax Trees

·         Intermediate forms

·         Global, stack and heap objects, and their addressing modes.

·      Stack implementation and calling conventions        

·        Instruction selection

·         Control flow analysis and optimization

·         Dataflow analysis and optimization using traditional dataflow.

·       Dataflow analysis and optimization using Static Single Assignment

·         Alias analysis

Course Structure: 

The course will have 4 homeworks, a software project, and four in-class exams, each 45 minutes.

Dates will be announced as we go along.  No discussion sections will be held. 

 

Scanning your homeworks into PDF:

For scanning your handwritten sheets into a single PDF, I strongly recommend using the free 
GeniusScan App, available on both iPhone and Android phones. The app takes pictures, for ‘

example of paper you point your camera to, improves those pictures by fixing their lighting,

angles, contrast, shadows etc, and then saves them as a PDF on your phone. You can them

email that PDF to yourself, and then upload it to Elms.

Please download this app right away, and practice scanning a couple of pages. Please make sure

you crop the images on the app to only include the page. Please make sure you edit all uploaded

pages by using "Filter" -> "Black and white" to improve image quality. You can crop and edit

using the app by clicking on the pen icon with stars around it. If you dispute your grade, please

contact the TAs during office hours.
