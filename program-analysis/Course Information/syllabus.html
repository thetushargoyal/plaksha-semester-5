Expected syllabus:

A brief outline of the syllabus is in the course information page. Here is the detailed expected syllabus in terms of the sections from the textbook (subject to change).  The final exam will be comprehensive, and will cover all the material covered in the entire semester.

Chapter 1: Introduction, 1.1 and 1.2.

Chapter 2: Introduction, 2.1, 2.2, 2.3 (excluding "Recognizing the longest match"), 2.4 (introduction only on page 24 and top of page 25). Conversions from regular expressions to NFAs, and from NFAs to DFAs are in syllabus in an ad-hoc manner, but formal procedures for the same in the textbook are not in syllabus.

Chapter 3: Introduction and 3.1 only.

Chapter 4: Introduction, 4.2 (upto and including "Positions").

Chapter 5: We will cover symbol tables very briefly, but not in a mathematical style, unlike the book.  You can read the first three pages of chapter 5, but ignore the math.

Chapter 6: Introduction, 6.1 (upto and including "Frame-resident variables").
